Gene D’Silva founder of Jeevan Dhara started his outreach work in 1998 in Asia’s second largest slum community namely Chita Camp, Trombay, Mumbai. For the first two years the goal was to get the HIV +ve patients accepted socially by the community. Identifying the needy by going door to door he provided food ration to 350 widows, school fees for children between the ages of 3 to 6 years of HIV +ve widows and providing medical assistance to the HIV +ve patients. OUR PROGRAMS

Danceclass
Dance classes started from 4th Feb 2016 with 9 children. Bollywood,Fusion Western etc. Seeing the enthusiasm among the slum children and how they would like to dance given the opportunity be it a wedding or any festival they would love to dance.
Medical Aid
With the parents being labor workers on daily wages it is very difficult to address any major illness if it came their way so there is a need for a Medical fund to make it easy for those who succumb to any major illnesses.
Football
Jeevan Dhara started its football training camps in the year 2011. The idea behind the football camp is to instill discipline in the life of these youngsters.The primary requirement to be a member of this camp is one should be a regular at school.
studyclass
It was noticed by our social workers the children who attended the local schools would not pay much attention to the material taught, as a result when they joined private schools after the 7th std they could not cope with the studies this led to a substantial increase in the number of drop outs.

vocational training
Jeevan Dhara observed there were many students who would drop out after their higher secondary. This was due to either lack of funds to pursue higher education, outright lack of interest in studies or they were just unaware about the opportunities available to them.
